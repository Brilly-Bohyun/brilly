Speaking at Open Source Summit in the US became an important milestone for me. Explaining technical work on stage is not only about transferring information. It also reveals what you believe is worth paying attention to.

I will add the exact talk title and session link once they are ready. For now, this post captures what I learned from preparing the talk and being in the room with the open source community.

## What preparation taught me

The hardest part was not adding more material. It was removing enough material for the core message to breathe.

A good talk is not the one that contains every detail. It is the one that helps people ask better questions afterward.

## What stayed with me

The energy of an open source conference continues outside the session rooms: hallway conversations, maintainers comparing hard-earned lessons, and questions that make you see your own work from a new angle.

What stayed with me most was how people from different backgrounds described similar problems in different language. That variety felt like the real engine of open source.

## What I want to improve next time

Next time, I want the examples and demo path to be even tighter. I also want to prepare a companion post and repository so the conversation can continue after the conference ends.
